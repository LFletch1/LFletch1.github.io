Exercise Evaluations Program

2019~2020

This project was the result of my time as a Kilgore Undergraduate Researcher. I utilized OpenPose, a body tracking software which can create a body structure from a video or image. The points generated by OpenPose were then used to analyze an individuals movements throughout a workout. The squat was chosen as the exercise of focus due to its ease of evaluation from a single angle. Additionally, I consulted with a biomechanics professor on what to look for in bad squat form. I created a GUI to allow the user to easily see where the system was detecting errors. After the analysis, the user is displayed with various stats from the exercise such as average repetition time, number of repetitions, and overall grade of the exercise. The program also maps the knee angle of each repetition to give the user the ability to visually compare each movement in the set. The project was not a traditional hypothesis based research project; however, I did learn many valuable lessons throughout the process.
